Trading Briefing

HX Trading Briefing — 25 Mar 2026

GP was soft over the last 7 days vs the same week last year: we made about £137k, down £25k or 16%, and most of the shortfall came from weaker direct sales while renewals partly offset it.

At a Glance

  • Direct Single hurt most — over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Direct Single GP fell £13k to £32k, with direct web sessions down 16%, search sessions down 31%, and GP per policy down 19%.
  • Direct Annual also weakened — over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Direct Annual GP fell £11k to £40k, as direct traffic fell and GP per policy dropped 14%.
  • Bronze leaked the most value — over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Bronze GP fell £20k to £29k because we sold 20% fewer policies and made 26% less on each one.
  • Partner referrals stayed soft — over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Partner Referral Single GP fell £5k to £12k and Partner Referral Annual GP fell £3k to £7k, mainly from weaker cruise-led volume.
  • Renewals helped — over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Renewals Annual GP rose £4k to £49k as renewed policies rose 18% to 1,322 and renewal rate improved to 47% from 30%.

Direct Single GP decline Recurring

✓ VERIFIEDIndependently verified by both OpenAI and Claude. Both models agree this finding is supported by the SQL evidence. The SQL evidence confirms Direct Single GP fell £13,063.59 (from £45,185.45 LY to £32,121.86 TY) as the largest negative driver. The analysis correctly identifies traffic decline (-16.2% total sessions), conversion weakening (session-to-search fell from 16.8% to 13.6%), and GP per policy deterioration (from £20.93 to £16.89), with cost decomposition showing UW costs rising from 46.0% to 50.3% of gross price.

Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Direct Single GP fell £13k to £32k. Traffic drove most of it, with direct web sessions down 16% and search sessions down 31%, but we also made less on each sale, with GP per policy down 19% to about £17.
The data clearly says this is both a traffic and margin problem. Mobile sessions were down hardest, session-to-search fell to 14% from 17%, and Bronze-heavy single-trip sales monetised worse as underwriter cost rose and GP margin fell to 29% from 37%. This has been down on 9 of the last 10 trading days.

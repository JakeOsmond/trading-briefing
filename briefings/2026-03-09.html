🚨 HX Insurance Trading Briefing — 8 March 2026

Margins drop as aggregator competition surges, but healthy annual volume growth is building future value — watch Single Trip losses.

The Biggest Movers

The sharpest hit is a £28,900/week ↓ bad drop in gross profit, driven by falling GP per policy — especially annuals sold via aggregators, where yields are down from £24.72 to £21.00 YoY. Within this, Annual Aggregator policies posted over 743 sales last week but delivered just -£6.36 GP per policy, with Elite and Deluxe lines in some non-medical tiers dipping below -£8 and on track to breach LTV safety (-£8/policy) if competition intensifies.

SELECT policy_type, distribution_channel, cover_level_name, medical_flag,
       SUM(policy_count) AS policies,
       SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt_ntu_comm AS FLOAT64)) AS total_gp,
       SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt_ntu_comm AS FLOAT64)) / NULLIF(SUM(policy_count), 0) AS avg_gp_per_policy
FROM `hx-data-production.commercial_finance.insurance_policies_new`
WHERE transaction_date BETWEEN '2026-03-01' AND '2026-03-08'
GROUP BY policy_type, distribution_channel, cover_level_name, medical_flag
ORDER BY total_gp ASC

The Funnel

Top-of-funnel widened sharply: visitors surged (18,062 vs 11,940 LY), but session-to-search conversion dropped to 48% vs 69% LY ↓ bad — driven by lower-intent, deal-hunting aggregator traffic. Search-to-book conversion improved to 79% vs 69% LY ↑ good, showing those who request a quote are now more committed; overall, the quality at purchase is up, but we’re addressing more "noise" at the top.

The Mix

Annuals now make up 48% of policies sold (up 14% YoY ↑ good); single-trips are up in count but dragging yield far below sustainable levels. The negative mix shift is rooted in a surge of price-comparison aggregator traffic, confirmed by market trends and our sales: aggregator share and cheap-tier policies are up, lowering blended margin by -£3.72/policy YoY (worth -£29k/week ↓ bad).

Market & Competitors

The insurance market is booming (+75% YoY search volume), but “compare travel insurance” (+805%) and “over 70 cover” (+211%) lead the growth, putting immense price pressure on HX — especially on aggregator channels, where Google Trends also shows brand search for HX itself is down 10%. Rivals are flooding aggregators and paid channels with price-led medical/annual offers, leveraging new FCA rules and airline travel surges (cheap flights up 132%). HX’s annual volume gains are winning renewal value but at the cost of yield; the fight is now on low-margin “cheap” tiers and medical lines.

What Changed Internally

Pricing docs were updated 9 March, following market-intelligence-inspired recommendations for more aggressive aggregator and medical annual pricing; this drove the recent margin squeeze but aligned with strategy to capture high-value renewal prospects.

Action Plan

  1. Stop negative-margin Single Trips on aggregators — These erode GP with no LTV upside; lock floor pricing and cut unprofitable lines immediately (worth ~£3k/week correction).
  2. Tighten aggregator annual pricing control — Hold losses within -£8/policy where possible, prioritizing yield over pointless volume in Elite and non-medical lines (risk exposure up to £10k/week if left unchecked).
  3. Double down on direct/partner annual medical — Channel medical/over-70 paid search, landing and SEO focus to Direct and Partner channels, maximizing high-yield annuals (capture an additional £5k+/week and safeguard renewal base).

Trading Briefing

HX Trading Briefing — 29 Mar 2026

GP was about £91k over the last 7 days vs the same week last year — down about £79k, roughly 46% worse, with most of the hit coming from Direct Single, Renewals and Direct Annual.

At a Glance

  • Direct Single — Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Direct Single GP fell by about £29k to about £21k, with sessions up on desktop and broadly flat on mobile but fewer people reaching a quote and desktop quote-to-book conversion also weakening.
  • Bronze cover — Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Bronze GP fell by about £28k to about £19k, largely because Direct Single Bronze volume and value both dropped hard.
  • Direct Annual — Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Direct Annual GP fell by about £22k to about £26k because we sold fewer annuals, which means we invested less into future renewal income.
  • Renewals — Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Renewals Annual GP fell by about £19k to about £33k, mainly because the expiry pool was 29% smaller even though renewal rate improved slightly.
  • Partner + aggregator singles — Over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, Partner Referral Single GP fell by about £8k to about £9k and Aggregator Single GP fell by about £2k to about £1k, so both single-trip acquisition channels were weaker.

Direct Single GP decline Recurring

✓ VERIFIEDIndependently verified by both OpenAI and Claude. Both models agree this finding is supported by the SQL evidence. The SQL evidence strongly supports the finding. Direct single GP fell from £49,054.33 (LY) to £20,524.95 (TY), confirming the £28,529.38 decline and down direction. The three-way hit is validated: policies dropped 39.1% (2,114 to 1,287), average GP per policy fell 31.3% (£23.20 to £15.95), and traffic quality weakened with session-to-search rates declining on both desktop (14.1% to 12.8%) and mobile (19.8% to 14.8%).

The data shows Direct Single GP fell by about £29k over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, down to about £21k, and this has been down on 9 of the last 10 days. Traffic was not the main problem because desktop sessions were up 34% and mobile sessions were up 2%, but fewer people got to quotes and desktop search-to-book fell from 46% to 32%, so we lost out on both conversion and value per sale.

SELECT
  SUM(policy_count) AS policies,
  SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt_ntu_comm AS FLOAT64)) AS gp
FROM `hx-data-production.insurance.insurance_trading_data`
WHERE distribution_channel = 'Direct'
  AND policy_type = 'Single'
  AND DATE(looker_trans_date) BETWEEN '2026-03-22' AND '2026-03-29';

Medical and senior mix shift inside Direct Single Emerging

Inside Direct Single over the last 7 days vs the same week last year, the higher-value medical and older-customer mix softened while lower-value non-medical Bronze business did more of the damage. That matters because medical screening journeys are still worth more when they convert, so part of the GP drop is that our traffic mix got worse as well as our funnel.

SELECT
  medical_split,
  SUM(policy_count) AS policies,
  SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt_ntu_comm AS FLOAT64)) AS gp,
  SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt AS FLOAT64)) / NULLIF(SUM(policy_count), 0) AS avg_price,
  SUM(CAST(total_gross_exc_ipt_ntu_comm AS FLOAT64)) / NULLIF(SUM(policy_count), 0) AS avg_gp_per_policy

# HX Group Business Unit Structure

Understanding the group structure explains how each domain relates to Group performance and which numbers are/aren't included.

```
Holiday Extras Group
├── UK Distribution (parking, hotels, lounges, fast track, transfers, car hire)
├── Europe (DACH, Benelux, Emerging Markets)
├── Insurance — HX Cover (regulated, FCA-authorised, separate P&L)
├── Adventures (Paultons Park, Warner Bros. Studio Tour partnerships)
├── AirParks (leased parking operations — Frankfurt, Munich, Amsterdam, Brussels)
└── HX Journeys (overlay — ground transport across UK + Europe, non-additive)
```

## Key Principles

- **Insurance has its own P&L** — it does not feed into UK Distribution numbers. Reported as a separate BU.
- **HX Journeys is non-additive** — car hire and transfers are already in UK/Europe totals. Journeys reports against its own targets, but the numbers are included in the parent BU.
- **AirParks is both a brand and an operator** — the AirParks brand sells via UK Distribution (distribution margin ~30%), while the operational entity runs leased venues (supplier margin ~70%).
- **Group Earnings** = Distribution GP + AirParks operational contribution — used when decisions affect both entities (e.g. pricing AirParks products).
- There is meaningful **customer overlap** between Insurance and UK Distribution — customers can hold products from both.

## Group Reporting

Weekly and monthly Group reporting aggregates all BUs. Primary Group metric: **Group GP vs forecast** and **Group GP vs last year**.

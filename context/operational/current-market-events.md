## Iran Conflict (Active since 28 Feb 2026)

US and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran on 28 February 2026. Iran responded with missile and drone attacks on Israel, US bases, UAE, and allied countries. Thousands of flights cancelled, Middle East airspace disrupted, Strait of Hormuz shipping rerouted.

### Impact on HX Insurance Trading
- **Annual volumes declining** — customers uncertain about how many trips they'll take this year, so buying single trip instead of committing to annual
- **Single trip volumes growing** — the opposite effect; demand for single trips is strong, especially short-lag bookings
- **Destination mix shift** — less Worldwide, more Europe. Customers avoiding conflict-adjacent destinations (Turkey, Cyprus at times taken off sale). Greece and Spain demand growing as alternatives
- **Search clickthrough rate spiked** — customers seeing prices and purchasing at much higher rates post-conflict (3-4pp above pre-conflict levels). Expected to normalise as travel sentiment settles
- **Pricing yielded up** — Europe Including and Worldwide Excluding GP per policy increased via pricing changes to capture higher willingness-to-pay
- **Aggregator instability** — Compare the Market (via idol platform) has had operational issues with new tech deployments, causing data quality problems
- **War risk exclusions** — standard travel insurance policies may not cover military action, government airspace closures. This is a market-wide issue, not HX-specific

### Interpretation Guidance
When the pipeline flags annual volume decline or GP pressure, this conflict is the primary driver. Do NOT recommend repricing annuals or flagging annual decline as a problem — it's a known market effect. Focus instead on:
- Single trip conversion and GP quality
- Destination mix implications for margin
- Whether short-lead bookings are holding (ancillary booking delay theory applies)
